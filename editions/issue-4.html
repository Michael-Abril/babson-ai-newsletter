Founder's Edge

Find your million-dollar AI opportunity in 3 steps

Everyone's building AI apps, but most are solving fake problems. Here's how to find real pain points that customers will actually pay for.

1. Map enterprise pain points [Claude]

Find specific workflows where companies waste time or money. Think beyond 'AI will make this faster' and find actual broken processes that keep managers awake at night.

Copy this prompt:

I'm researching B2B AI opportunities. For the industry [INSERT INDUSTRY], list 10 specific daily workflows that cost companies serious time or money. For each workflow, explain: 1) What manual steps are involved 2) How much time/money gets wasted 3) Why existing solutions don't work well 4) What data/inputs are available to automate this. Focus on processes that happen multiple times per day and involve knowledge workers.

2. Check if people actually want this [Perplexity]

Check if people are actually searching for solutions to these problems. Real demand shows up in search volume, existing tool adoption, and people complaining on Twitter.

Copy this prompt:

Research the market demand for [WORKFLOW FROM STEP 1]. Find: 1) Monthly search volume for related keywords 2) Existing tools/software that partially solve this 3) Pricing of current solutions 4) Common complaints in reviews of existing tools 5) Recent funding rounds or acquisitions in this space. Include specific numbers and sources.

3. Design your MVP scope [Claude]

Define the smallest version that solves the core problem. Most AI startups fail by building too much instead of solving one thing perfectly for paying customers.

Copy this prompt:

Help me scope an MVP for [YOUR AI SOLUTION IDEA]. The target workflow is [WORKFLOW FROM STEP 1]. Design a minimal viable product that: 1) Solves just the core problem (no nice-to-haves) 2) Needs minimal custom data to start working 3) Can be built by 1-2 people in 6-8 weeks 4) Has clear success metrics. Provide: MVP feature list, tech stack recommendations, expected development timeline, and 3 ways to measure product-market fit in the first 90 days.

This process takes 4 hours but can save you 6 months building the wrong thing and running out of runway.

AI in Your Business

3 AI workflows that actually save money

Competitor intel automation [Claude]

Stop manually checking competitor websites and social media. Set up automated monitoring that tells you exactly when to pivot your strategy or pricing.

Copy this prompt:

Create a competitor monitoring system for my startup. My main competitors are [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2], [COMPETITOR 3]. For each competitor, create a weekly monitoring checklist that covers: 1) Product/feature updates 2) Pricing changes 3) New partnerships or integrations 4) Marketing campaigns and messaging shifts 5) Job postings that reveal where they're heading. For each item, tell me exactly where to find this info and what questions to ask to turn the data into actionable insights for my business.

Customer feedback analysis [Claude]

Turn support tickets, reviews, and user feedback into product roadmap priorities. Find patterns humans miss and spot your highest-impact improvements before customers churn.

Copy this prompt:

Analyze this customer feedback data and extract actionable insights: [PASTE YOUR FEEDBACK DATA: reviews, support tickets, surveys]. Provide: 1) Top 5 most frequent complaint categories with exact quotes 2) Feature requests ranked by frequency and potential revenue impact 3) Churn risk indicators I should watch for 4) 3 quick wins that would solve multiple customer problems 5) Draft email responses for the most common issues. Present everything as a prioritized action plan for the next 30 days.

Content repurposing at scale [Claude]

Take one piece of content and turn it into weeks of social posts, blog articles, and email newsletters. Perfect for lean startups with zero marketing budget.

Copy this prompt:

Repurpose this content across multiple formats: [PASTE YOUR ORIGINAL CONTENT: blog post, presentation, video transcript]. Create: 1) 10 LinkedIn posts (mix of tips, insights, and questions) 2) 5 Twitter thread ideas with first tweets written out 3) 3 email newsletter sections 4) 1 long-form blog outline with key points 5) 5 Instagram caption ideas. Keep my original tone and key messages. Make each piece feel native to its platform, not like a copy-paste job.
