Founder's Edge

Research any competitor in 10 minutes

You need to know your competition inside and out, but who has time to dig through hundreds of pages when you're prepping for BEF or testing ideas on campus? Let AI do the heavy lifting.

1. Upload everything you can find [Google NotebookLM]

Grab their website, annual reports, press releases, product docs. Throw it all into NotebookLM (it's free and now built into Gemini).

Copy this prompt:

Analyze these documents about [COMPETITOR NAME] and create a comprehensive competitive analysis covering: 1) Their business model and revenue streams 2) Key strengths and weaknesses 3) Pricing strategy 4) Target customers 5) Recent moves 6) Potential weaknesses my startup could exploit. Format as an executive summary with specific data points and actionable insights.

2. Find the gaps [Claude]

Ask AI to identify what they're NOT doing: that's where your opportunity lives.

Copy this prompt:

Based on this competitive research, identify 5 specific gaps or unmet needs in [COMPETITOR NAME]'s offering that a startup could address. For each gap, explain: 1) Why they haven't filled it yet 2) What customer pain point it represents 3) How difficult it would be for them to address 4) The potential market size for solving this gap. Rank by opportunity size.

3. Build your differentiation strategy [Claude]

Turn those gaps into your competitive advantage and positioning strategy.

Copy this prompt:

Create a differentiation strategy for my startup [YOUR COMPANY] competing against [COMPETITOR NAME]. Based on the gaps identified, develop: 1) A unique value proposition that directly addresses their weaknesses 2) Key messaging points that highlight our advantages 3) Specific features or services we should prioritize 4) A go-to-market strategy that exploits their blind spots. Make it specific and actionable.

Save 20+ hours of research and get insights most founders miss completely.

AI in Your Business

Sales & Outreach: Fill Your Pipeline

Cold emails that actually get replies [Claude]

Stop sending generic pitches that scream 'mass email'. AI has analyzed thousands of successful cold emails and knows exactly what works.

Copy this prompt:

Write a cold email for [YOUR COMPANY] reaching out to [TARGET ROLE] at [COMPANY TYPE] companies. The email should: 1) Reference something specific about their company or industry 2) Lead with a relevant insight or stat they'd care about 3) Make one clear, valuable offer 4) Include a soft CTA that's easy to say yes to 5) Be under 100 words. Make it sound human, not salesy. Include 3 different subject line options.

LinkedIn outreach without being annoying [Claude]

Everyone's tired of 'I'd love to connect and explore synergies' messages. Stand out by actually adding value from the first touch.

Copy this prompt:

Create a LinkedIn connection request and follow-up sequence for [YOUR COMPANY] targeting [TARGET ROLE]. The sequence should: 1) Initial connection request (under 200 characters) that references their recent post/achievement 2) First follow-up message that shares a relevant resource or insight 3) Second follow-up with a specific, low-commitment ask 4) Third follow-up if still no response. Make each message valuable on its own, not just asking for something.

Follow-up sequences that close deals [Claude]

Most deals happen after the 5th touchpoint, but most founders give up after 2. AI never forgets to follow up and knows exactly what to say.

Copy this prompt:

Create a 6-email follow-up sequence for prospects who showed initial interest in [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE] but went quiet. Each email should: 1) Have a different angle/reason for following up 2) Provide new value (case study, insight, resource) 3) Address a different potential objection 4) Include social proof relevant to their situation 5) End with a clear, specific next step. Space them 3, 7, 14, 21, and 30 days apart. Include subject lines for each.
