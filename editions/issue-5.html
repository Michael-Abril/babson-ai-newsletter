Founder's Edge

Nail your investor pitch in 3 hours

Most student pitches fail because they don't tell a story investors care about. Whether you're prepping for the Babson Entrepreneurship Forum, Rocket Pitch, or meeting Boston VCs, here's how to build a deck that gets meetings.

1. Write your core narrative [Claude]

Start with the story that connects problem to solution to market opportunity.

Copy this prompt:

I'm pitching my startup [STARTUP NAME] which [ONE SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]. Help me craft a compelling narrative for investors. My target market is [TARGET MARKET], the main problem is [PROBLEM], and my solution is [SOLUTION]. Write a 2-minute pitch story that follows this structure: Hook (surprising stat/story) → Problem (pain point) → Solution (your product) → Market size → Traction → Ask. Make it conversational and avoid buzzwords.

2. Build your financial projections [Claude]

Create realistic revenue forecasts that don't make investors roll their eyes.

Copy this prompt:

Help me build 3-year financial projections for [STARTUP NAME]. My business model: [DESCRIBE REVENUE MODEL]. Current metrics: [CURRENT REVENUE/USERS/GROWTH]. Comparable companies: [2-3 SIMILAR COMPANIES]. Create month-by-month projections for Year 1 and quarterly for Years 2-3. Include: Revenue, Customer acquisition cost, Customer count, Monthly recurring revenue (if applicable), Key assumptions. Make it realistic for a [STAGE] startup and explain your reasoning.

3. Practice with brutal feedback [Claude]

Get ripped apart by AI before investors do it in person.

Copy this prompt:

I'm going to pitch my startup to you. You're a tough Series A investor who's seen 1000 pitches. Here's my pitch: [PASTE YOUR FULL PITCH]. Now tear it apart. What are the 3 biggest weaknesses? What questions will investors ask that I can't answer? What assumptions am I making that seem unrealistic? What would make you not invest? Be brutally honest and specific.

Saves 20+ hours of pitch iteration and gets you investor-ready in one afternoon.

AI in Your Business

Pitching & Fundraising Workflows

Investor Research in Minutes [Perplexity]

Know exactly what each investor cares about before you email them. Perfect for connecting with Babson's alumni network or Boston VCs.

Copy this prompt:

Research [INVESTOR NAME] at [VC FIRM]. I need to know: Recent investments in my space ([YOUR INDUSTRY]), their typical check size and stage, what they've said about [RELEVANT TREND/TECH], their background, and any portfolio companies similar to [YOUR STARTUP]. Find their recent interviews, tweets, or blog posts. Give me 3 specific conversation starters I could use when pitching them.

Competitive Analysis That Impresses [Claude]

Build a competitive view that shows you actually understand your market.

Copy this prompt:

Create a full competitive analysis for [YOUR STARTUP] in the [YOUR MARKET] space. Compare these competitors: [LIST 5-7 COMPETITORS]. For each, analyze: Business model, pricing, target customer, key features, funding raised, strengths/weaknesses, market positioning. Then create a 2x2 positioning map showing where we fit. Finally, explain our differentiation and why we'll win. Make this investor-ready.

Due Diligence Data Room Prep [ChatGPT]

Get organized before investors start asking for documents you don't have.

Copy this prompt:

I'm raising [FUNDING STAGE] for my [INDUSTRY] startup [COMPANY NAME]. Create a complete due diligence checklist organized by category: Legal documents, Financial records, Product/Tech, Team, Customers, IP, etc. For each document, explain why investors want it and flag any that are critical vs nice-to-have. Also suggest 3 tools for organizing our data room and timeline for prep.
