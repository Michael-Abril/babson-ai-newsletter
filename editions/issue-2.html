Founder's Edge

Build your AI study system (power-user edition)

Most students use NotebookLM like ChatGPT - they ask basic questions and get basic answers. Here's how to actually leverage it. These prompts use features 90% of users don't know exist.

1. Create a specification note that trains the AI [NotebookLM]

Before asking any questions, create a 'spec note' that tells NotebookLM how you learn and what you need. This shapes every future response in that notebook.

Copy this prompt:

Create a specification note for this notebook: - Course: [COURSE NAME with professor] - Exam format: [multiple choice / essay / case study / etc.] - Professor's grading emphasis: [paste key phrases from syllabus] - Concepts I struggle with: [list 2-3 topics] - How I learn best: [examples / analogies / step-by-step / visual] - Output format I prefer: [bullets / narrative / Q&A] Use this context for all future responses. Prioritize what the professor emphasizes.

2. Generate a custom study guide report [NotebookLM]

Use the Custom Reports feature to create a comprehensive study guide. This isn't a basic summary - it's a structured document you could actually hand to someone else.

Copy this prompt:

Generate a CUSTOM REPORT: Comprehensive Study Guide Structure: 1. Executive Summary - The 5 things I absolutely must know (half page) 2. Concept Breakdown - Each major topic with: - Core definition in plain English - Real-world example I can reference in essays - How it connects to other concepts 3. Likely Exam Questions - Based on what the professor emphasized 4. Model Answers - How a top student would answer each 5. Common Mistakes - Where students typically lose points 6. Quick Reference - One-page cheat sheet format Tone: Like a senior who got an A explaining to a freshman Length: Detailed (2,000+ words)

3. Run an exam simulation with grading [NotebookLM]

Don't just review - test yourself. This prompt creates a realistic exam, grades your answers, and teaches you what you missed.

Copy this prompt:

Act as my professor for [COURSE NAME]. Create a realistic practice exam: Part 1: Concept Check (5 questions) - Multiple choice with 4 options each - Include one 'trick' question the professor would use - After I answer, explain why wrong answers are wrong Part 2: Application (3 questions) - Short answer requiring specific examples from readings - Grade on a 10-point scale with specific feedback Part 3: Synthesis (1 question) - Essay question connecting multiple concepts - Provide a grading rubric before I answer - After I answer, grade me and show what an A+ answer includes Start with Part 1. Wait for my answers before moving on.

These prompts took me from using NotebookLM as a fancy search bar to actually understanding my material. The exam simulation alone is worth the 15 minutes of setup.

Real talk - my actual setup: One notebook per class. Everything labeled by date. Before each class I create a quick summary of the readings - not to skip them, this supplements the actual work. I review it right before class and right after. Before exams: I select only the sources being tested and generate multiple study guides, not just one. NotebookLM misses things, so I run 2-3 different prompts to catch everything. Then I create an Audio Overview and listen walking to the exam.

AI in Your Business

NotebookLM for Your Startup

Competitive intelligence brief [NotebookLM]

Upload competitor websites, pitch decks, reviews, and industry reports. Get a structured analysis that looks like it came from a consulting firm.

Copy this prompt:

Generate a CUSTOM REPORT: Competitive Analysis Brief Context: I'm building [YOUR STARTUP - one sentence] Structure: 1. Market Landscape - Who are the players and what's total market size 2. Feature Matrix - Table comparing what each competitor offers 3. Pricing Analysis - How each charges and their positioning 4. Gap Analysis - What NO ONE is doing well (my opportunity) 5. Differentiation Strategy - How I should position against each 6. Risks - What could go wrong if I enter this market Tone: McKinsey consultant briefing a CEO Format: Executive summary up front, details after Length: Detailed with comparison tables

Investor meeting one-pager [NotebookLM]

Upload the VC's website, portfolio page, partner bios, and any interviews. Get personalized talking points for your specific meeting.

Copy this prompt:

Generate a CUSTOM REPORT: Investor Intelligence Brief Meeting: [INVESTOR NAME] at [FIRM NAME] Structure: 1. Firm Profile - Investment thesis, typical check size, stage focus 2. This Partner's Background - Their history and what they care about 3. Recent Investments - What they've funded in last 12 months 4. Portfolio Synergies - Their companies I could partner with or reference 5. Likely Questions - What they'll probably ask based on their thesis 6. My Talking Points - How to connect my startup to their pattern 7. Things to Avoid - Red flags based on their public statements Tone: Internal strategy memo before a big meeting Length: 1,500 words, scannable with clear headers

Customer research synthesis [NotebookLM]

Upload interview transcripts, survey responses, support tickets, or reviews. Get patterns and exact quotes you can use in your pitch.

Copy this prompt:

Generate a CUSTOM REPORT: Voice of Customer Analysis Structure: 1. Top Pain Points - Ranked by how often they appear (with frequency) 2. Exact Quotes - The specific words customers use (for my landing page) 3. Jobs to Be Done - What are they actually trying to accomplish 4. Current Solutions - What they use now and why it fails them 5. Willingness to Pay - Any signals about budget or price sensitivity 6. Persona Clusters - Group similar customers together 7. Messaging Recommendations - What to say based on their language Tone: Product manager presenting findings to founders Format: Highlight direct quotes in each section Length: Detailed, with quotes pulled directly from sources

How I use this for Varity: All my market research, customer surveys, and competitive intel lives in NotebookLM - labeled like folders so I can toggle between topics. I keep roadmap files in there and update them as I make progress. When I need a deck, brief, or any document, I use Deep Research mode. Everything it generates is specific to MY startup because it's pulling from MY data. No generic advice.
